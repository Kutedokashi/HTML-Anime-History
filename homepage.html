Start of the anime

Like manga, anime is a form of entertainment that has been able to break all
records within audiovisual disciplines.

It appears for the first time in Japan at the beginning of the 20th century
as a way of dealing with the giant Disney. These cartoons in the form of Japanese
cartoons are the representation on the big screen of many of the famous stories
that we can read in the manga.

The first anime short film was created in 1917 by the Japanese animator
Junichi Kouchi. However, we would have to wait for 1960 to begin to enjoy
the first quality anime and the future influence of world-class productions
such as Spirited Away or Mazinger Z.

Both manga and anime can be categorized into a wide variety of genres based
on particular demographics and subject matter. The fans and faithful followers
of these cultures are known under the popular term otakus. You can easily
recognize them in manga parlors for having extensive knowledge of classic
manga and cosplay.

Development of the industry and international rise

In the course of the crisis, the manga and anime industry became popular in the
country , thus laying the foundations for the development of its own animated style
that occurred around the middle of the 20th century; it was around this time that
Toei Studios emerged, animation film producer, one of the key figures in the history
of anime; This company was a pioneer in animation in Japan, providing various
productions that allowed the advancement of animation in the country. The company's
first animation was Koneko no rakugaki , a thirteen-minute short film published in 1957;
Over the next decade the company grew with a focus on feature film development. Around
this same period, other companies emerged such as Mushi Pro, a production company that
made the animation of Tetsuwan Atomu (Astro Boy) by Osamu Tezuka, mangaka and animator,
one of the most important artists of the Japanese animated industry of the 20th century.

Why is anime so popular nowadays?

The anime industry has grown considerably in Latin America
and the United States in recent years. Titles like "My Hero Academia" and
"Kimetsu no Yaiba" have become huge popular phenomena through social networks,
with millions of fans waiting for the premiere of their new chapters and
commenting on the plot in different publications.

The growth of social networks, the different streaming services, and a
great accesibility capacity for the different anime productions
available, has led millions of users to join the fanaticism of Japanese
animation, a very different panorama from what was seen in the early
days of the internet, where it was very difficult to find anime episodes
online.
